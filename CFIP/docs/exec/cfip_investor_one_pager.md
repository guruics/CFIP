# CFIP — Investor One-Pager

## The Problem

Regulated organizations run critical financial workflows across siloed systems. The real risk is not moving data—it is **implicit, undocumented financial logic** spread across spreadsheets and people. Automation without governance increases audit and compliance risk.

## The Solution

**CFIP** is a **governed planning control plane** that converts business intent into **explicit, auditable workflow plans** before execution. CFIP does not execute systems; it **defines what is allowed, when, and why**.

## Why Now

- Increased regulatory scrutiny
- Rising pressure to automate finance operations
- AI adoption without adequate guardrails

CFIP enables safe automation by making intent explicit first.

## Lighthouse Use Case

**Regulated financial reconciliation** (e.g., Medicaid remittance-to-deposit reconciliation):
- Multi-system
- High dollar impact
- High audit exposure
- Poorly served by ad-hoc tools

## What Makes This Defensible

- Policy-aware planning semantics
- Capability registries as institutional memory
- Discovery-to-production evolution loop
- Domain ownership hyperscalers avoid

## Category

## Business Value

- Reduced audit risk
- Faster close and reconciliation cycles
- Safer automation
- Institutionalized financial logic

**CFIP turns regulated financial intent into governed, auditable workflow plans—starting with reconciliation and expanding to safe automation.**
